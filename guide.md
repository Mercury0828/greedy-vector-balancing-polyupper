## §The Idea

**Current state.** Czerwiński et al. (arXiv:2606.17991, "Greedy Vector Balancing", 2026-06-16) give the **first length-independent finite-universe bound** for deterministic greedy Euclidean vector balancing: `G(T) ≤ (2/δ_T)^{d-1}`, matched only by an `Ω(√d/δ_T)` lower bound (their Lemma 1.4, via `d` **orthogonal** copies of a 2D near-parallel gadget — i.e. *additive/quadrature* accumulation). Per the paper, the authors leave the tightness open: *"We do not know if the estimate in Theorem 1.2 is tight"* (**confirmed verbatim in the full-text HTML on 2026-06-22 by the deepdive author; Phase-0 re-confirm at submission**). The gap is open.

**The gap / our claim.** We claim the polynomial side: for **constant (or `1/poly(d)`) `δ_T`**, `G(T) = poly(d, 1/δ_T)`. If true, the `(2/δ_T)^{d-1}` ceiling is a loose artifact of the convex-absorbing analysis, and greedy is in fact polynomially bounded for well-spread universes.

**Why this path is plausible (the boundary argument that makes it stand up).** Three structural facts (all from the GPT-5 Pro analysis; each is a Phase-0 verification target, not yet a frozen lemma):
1. **No exponential ill-conditioning from independent vectors.** Any independent basis `B ⊆ T` has `σ_min(B) ≥ δ_T/√r` (the dual rows of `B^{-1}` have norm = `1/dist(b_i, span(B∖b_i)) ≤ 1/δ_T`, so `‖B^{-1}‖_F ≤ √r/δ_T`). So no Klee–Minty "each layer slightly tilted, product explodes" chain can exist among independent directions; an exponential construction would have to exploit **exact dependencies**, not near-dependencies.
2. **High-radius growth is confined to a proper subspace.** Only directions with `|⟨x,t⟩| < 1/2` can increase `‖x‖` (unit step). If `N(x) := {t∈T : |⟨x,t⟩|<1/2}` spans the full `r`-dim space, pick a basis `B⊆N(x)`: then `‖B^⊤x‖₂ ≤ √r/2`, so `‖x‖ ≤ ‖B^{-T}‖₂·‖B^⊤x‖₂ ≤ r/(2δ_T)`. Hence **`‖x‖ > r/(2δ_T) ⟹ span N(x) ⊊ span T`** — at high radius the growth directions live in a proper `T`-subspace.
3. **Constant `δ_T` *may* force only polynomially many directions:** a 2025 column-number theorem (arXiv:2510.20301) bounds pairwise non-collinear columns by `O(d⁴·κ_A)` where `κ_A` is the **circuit-imbalance measure** (⚠️ **a *different* coherence parameter than our `δ_T`** — the paper does **not** relate `κ_A` to the min-distance-to-span `δ_T`). So **fact-3 is a *candidate* lever, NOT established**: Phase 0 must check whether `κ_A` can be bounded by `1/poly(δ_T)` for our `T` at all — **if not, fact-3 does not apply and is dropped** (the `(★)`/HS2 route does not depend on it). Do not cite `O(d⁴/δ_T)` as fact.

**The load-bearing technique (concrete, falsifiable) — this IS the open crux `(★)`.** What the anchor *does* establish (Phase-0 confirm the exact lemma) is that **greedy is bounded by the radius of a convex `T`-absorbing / `T`-projective body `K_T`** — that is their foundation for bounding greedy sequences. From there GPT-Pro *reconstructed* (⚠️ **NOT verbatim in the paper — a Phase-0 derivation task**) a support-function iteration `h_{K_n}(y) = max_{t∈T}( h_{K_{n-1}}(π_t y) + |⟨y,t⟩| ) = sup Σ_j ‖y_j − y_{j-1}‖` (`y_j = π_{t_j} y_{j-1}`), which *would* reduce a poly bound on `G(T)` to:
> **(★ the crux), route HS1 (TV):** for the finite hyperplane-projection family `{π_t : t∈T}` with `δ_T ≥ δ`, prove every orthogonal-projection product trajectory has total variation `Σ_j ‖y_j − y_{j-1}‖ ≤ poly(d, 1/δ)·‖y_0‖`.

🔴 **Two honest caveats Phase 0 must settle before HS1 is trusted:** (a) the support-function iteration identity above is **our reconstruction, not stated in the anchor** — Phase 0 must either *derive* it from the paper's `K_T` machinery or **drop HS1 in favor of HS2**; (b) the **qualitative** bounded variation of finite orthogonal-projection trajectories is a *candidate* prior fact (arXiv:1603.00854, on products of matrices/projections — **Phase-0 verify it actually gives the qualitative BV we need; the route does not strictly require it**). Because of (a)/(b), treat **HS2 as a co-equal (arguably more solid) primary route, not a fallback**:
> **(★ the crux), route HS2 (subspace-switch charging):** at high radius (fact 2, which rests only on the verifiable `P1/P2`) the growth directions sit in a *proper* `T`-subspace; prove that switching the growth-subspace must pay a summable norm drop, capping the number of switches and hence `G(T)` at `poly(d,1/δ_T)`. HS2 leans on `P1,P2` (numerically checkable, no unverified anchor machinery), so it is the safer of the two attacks.

`(★)` = "HS1 or HS2 yields `poly(d,1/δ_T)`" is the single open crux Phase 0 de-risks and the pivot trigger if refuted.

**Boundary (what keeps novelty / what we explicitly do NOT claim).** (a) `δ_T` must be **constant or `1/poly(d)`** — for *exponentially small* `δ_T` the question is uninteresting (the anchor's own `Ω(√d/δ_T)` already gives a large bound). (b) We work in the **anchor's `[-1,1]T` scaled model** (their `G(T)`), not a unit-step-only model. (c) If `(★)` is **true**, there is **no** exponential greedy-vs-optimal separation (greedy is itself poly) — the contribution is the *tightness resolution* + an exponential upper-bound *improvement* on a brand-new (2026) primitive, **not** a separation. (d) We do **not** claim anything about the *lower-bound* direction; that is the sibling idea.

**Nearest neighbors + differences.** See §Differentiation and §Literature.

> 🔧 **Three GPT-Pro model corrections to honor from day 0** (they invalidate naive framings): (i) the model is `[-1,1]T` *scaled*, not unit-step-only; (ii) the anchor's `T`-chain (flag of subspaces) is a **convex-absorbing construct, NOT the greedy trajectory's level-exit decomposition** — do not argue as if a trajectory marches through `d-1` multiplicative level-exits; (iii) the anchor's Lemma 1.4 is an **existence** lower bound (∃ T), not a per-`T` bound, and there is **no** `G({0,1}^d) ≥ d^{d/2}` claim in the paper (only the upper bound `d^{d²/2}`).

## §Formal Framework / Notation

- Ambient: `ℝ^d`, Euclidean norm `‖·‖`. Finite `T ⊆ S^{d-1}` (unit sphere); `r = dim span(T) ≤ d`.
- **`δ_T`** := minimum, over `t∈T` and over subsets `U⊆T` with `t∉span(U)`, of `dist(t, span(U))` (equivalently the min nonzero distance from a `T`-vector to a subspace spanned by other `T`-vectors). Regime of interest: **`δ_T ≥ c` constant, or `δ_T ≥ 1/poly(d)`**.
- **Greedy** (anchor's rule), `[-1,1]T` scaled model: vectors `t_1,t_2,… ∈ T` arrive (adversarial, with repetition; signed/scaled coefficients in `[-1,1]` per the anchor's `G(T)` definition — **Phase 0 confirm the exact scaled-model statement**); maintain `S_k = Σ_{i≤k} s_i t_i`; at step `k` choose `s_k` so that `s_k t_k` has **non-positive** inner product with `S_{k-1}`. `G(T) = sup ‖S_k‖` over all greedy sequences.
- Unit-step norm recursion (sanity tool): `‖S_k‖² = ‖S_{k-1}‖² − 2|⟨S_{k-1},t_k⟩| + 1`.
- Projection operators: `π_t` = orthogonal projection onto `t^⊥` (the anchor's projective-closure operator); product trajectory `y_j = π_{t_j} y_{j-1}`.
- Convex objects: `K_T` = minimal convex `T`-absorbing body; `h_K` = its support function. `G(T) ≤ radius(K_T)`.

## §Theory / Construction Program

Each item: **claim shape / proof skeleton / verification / risk-if-it-collapses.** The **load-bearing crux is HS1/HS2**; everything else is substrate or supporting.

- **P-targets (the substrate to freeze in Phase 0 / early attack — "use freely once verified"):**
  - `P1` `σ_min(B) ≥ δ_T/√r` for independent `B⊆T`. *Verify:* direct (dual-row norm); numeric check on random/adversarial `T`. *Risk:* essentially a definition-chase; low.
  - `P2` `‖x‖ > r/(2δ_T) ⟹ span N(x) ⊊ span T`. *Verify:* the basis argument above; numeric check. *Risk:* low; if the constant is off it only shifts the radius threshold.
  - `P3` (**CANDIDATE, may be dropped**) non-collinear directions `≤ O(d⁴·κ_A)`, `κ_A` = circuit-imbalance measure (arXiv:2510.20301 — confirmed by the deepdive author 2026-06-22 that the bound is `O(d⁴·κ_A)` and `κ_A` is circuit imbalance, **NOT** the min-distance `δ_T`). *Verify in Phase 0:* whether `κ_A ≤ 1/poly(δ_T)` holds for our `T` at all — **if not, P3 is dropped.** *Risk:* none to the main result — HS1/HS2 do not depend on P3.
  - `P4` `G(T) ≤ radius(K_T)` (the convex `T`-absorbing-body bound — **this IS the paper's foundation; Phase-0 confirm the exact lemma**) PLUS the support-function ITERATION identity (**OUR reconstruction, NOT in the paper — confirmed absent by the deepdive author 2026-06-22**). *Verify in Phase 0:* derive the iteration from `K_T`, OR abandon HS1 and run HS2 (which needs only `P1,P2`). *Risk:* if the iteration can't be derived, the `(★)` route = HS2 only (still viable).
  - `P5` (absorbing-neighborhood lemma, GPT-Pro, hand-checked once by the deepdive author): for a fixed convex `H`-absorbing `K⊆B(0,R_H)`, `K+rB₂` is `H`-absorbing; hence a greedy phase using only rank-`q` directions grows additively `‖x_out‖ ≤ R_H + ‖x_in‖`, `R_H ≤ (2/δ_T)^{q-1}`. *Verify:* the **ledger must still log a full hand-written proof audit** — do NOT treat the external GPT-Pro verdict as a literature fact. *Use:* rules out the obvious "recursive fixed-rank amplifier" obstruction to a poly bound (mild evidence for B). *Risk:* none for B; it is a one-directional helper.

- **HS1 — the crux (route A: total variation).** *Claim shape:* `sup Σ_j ‖y_j − y_{j-1}‖ ≤ poly(d,1/δ_T)·‖y_0‖` over all orthogonal-projection product trajectories in `{π_t : t∈T}`, `δ_T ≥ δ`. *Proof skeleton (to be ORIGINATED BY THE ATTACKER, not by Claude):* leverage `P1/P2` + the known qualitative boundedness (1603.00854) to a `δ`-explicit polynomial quantitative bound; candidate angles: potential/energy argument on `‖y_j‖²` with a `δ`-quantified contraction-or-charge per step; spectral-gap of the projection semigroup; combinatorial bound on "productive" steps via `P2`. *Verification:* numeric — adversarially search trajectories maximizing TV at constant `δ`, fit growth vs `d`. *Risk-if-collapses:* if TV is provably exponential at constant `δ` → `(★)` false → strong evidence for A → **pivot** (human gate).
- **HS2 — the crux (route B: subspace-switch charging).** *Claim shape:* at radius `> r/(2δ_T)` the growth-subspace `span N(x)` is proper; each switch of the growth-subspace costs a norm drop `≥ g(δ_T,d)`; hence the number of switches (and the norm) is `poly`. *Proof skeleton (attacker-originated):* define a subspace potential; show monotone charging. *Verification:* numeric switch-count vs `d`. *Risk:* same pivot trigger as HS1; HS1 and HS2 are two attacks on the same crux — running both in parallel is encouraged.
- **HS3 — assemble.** Combine the verified substrate + HS1/HS2 into `G(T) ≤ poly(d,1/δ_T)`, in the anchor's scaled model, for constant/`1/poly(d)` `δ_T`. *Verification:* end-to-end numeric (adversarial `T`, measure `G` vs `d` at fixed `δ`). *Risk:* constants/exponent of the poly — acceptable as long as it is `poly` (SODA wants the *qualitative* exponential→poly resolution; a clean exponent is bonus).

**Load-bearing assumption, stated as the death/life point:** `(★)` (HS1≡HS2 crux) — *the projection-total-variation / subspace-switch bound is polynomial at constant `δ_T`*. This is exactly what Phase 0 must de-risk (adversarial sim) before any attack rounds are spent, and it is the pivot trigger if refuted.
